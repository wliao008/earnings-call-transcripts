John Swygert

Thanks, Mark. And good afternoon everyone. We had another strong quarter and we're pleased with our results. Net sales increased 16.1% to $211.3 million. Comparable store sales increased 3.5% versus an increase of 7.8% in the second quarter of last year. The increase in comparable store sales was driven by an increase in the number of transactions.

We opened eight new stores in the quarter ending with 216 stores in 19 states versus 187 stores in 16 states last year. Our new stores continue to perform in line or above our expectations and we remain pleased with the productivity of our store base.

Gross profit increased 19.6% to $83.8 million and gross margin increased 120 basis points to 39.7%. The increase in gross margin was driven by reduced transportation and distribution center costs as a percent of net sales and a slightly higher merchandise margin.

SG&A expenses increased 16.5% to $57.7 million in the quarter and included $260,000 of transaction related expenses. The increase was primarily related to higher selling expenses from our new stores opened over the past year, increased sales volumes as well as public company expenses. As a percent of net sales, SG&A increased 10 basis points to 27.3% in the second quarter.

Pre-opening expenses were $2.0 million versus $1.9 million in the second quarter of last year. The slight increase was related to timing differences and new stores.

Operating income in the quarter increased 30.5% to $22 million and operating margins increased 110 basis points to 10.4%. Our effective tax rate was 36% versus 36.9% in the second quarter of last year. The decline in tax rate was primarily the result of additional tax credits realized in the quarter.

Net income increased 106.8% to $13.1 million and earnings per diluted share increased 75% to $0.21 from $0.12 last year. Excluding transaction related expenses, adjusted net income increased 65.5% to $13.3 million and adjusted earnings per diluted share increased 40% to $0.21 from $0.15 last year.

Looking at a couple of other non-GAAP metrics. EBITDA increased 28.4% to $24.6 million and adjusted EBITDA increased 27% to $28.6 million. At the end of the second quarter we had $30.7 million in cash and no outstanding borrowings on our $100 million revolving credit facility. During the quarter we paid down $1.3 million of our term loan debt and ended the quarter with total debt of $197.7 million versus $224.5 million the same time last year. Capital expenditures were $5.2 million in the second quarter versus $3.5 million in the same period last year.

Turning to our outlook for fiscal 2016. We are raising our full year guidance to the following: Total net sales of $880 million to $885 million, up from our previous guidance of $868 million to $878 million; comparable store sales growth of 2.5% to 3%, up from our previous guidance of 1.5% to 2.5%; the opening of 28 to 32 new stores and no planned closures which is consistent with our previous guidance; operating income of $96 million to $98 million which is up from previous guidance of $93 million to $96 million; net income per diluted share of $0.87 to $0.89, up from our previous guidance of $0.84 to $0.86 per diluted share. This assumes a full year effective tax rate of 38.2% versus our previous assumption of 38.8%. It also assumes a diluted share count of 62.5 million shares which is unchanged from previous guidance.

Excluding transaction related expenses, we expect adjusted net income per diluted share to be between $0.88 and $0.90. This is up from our previous range of $0.85 to $0.87. Finally, capital expenditures are still expected to be $16.5 million to $17.5 million range.

As we've previously discussed, we faced our most challenging sales comparison of the year in the fourth quarter with a comparable store sales increase of 14% on a two-year stack basis. As a reminder, record warm weather in last year's fourth quarter resulted in minimal store closures and lost business. We obviously cannot control or predict the weather but we want to remind everyone of the favorable conditions in the past two holiday quarters.

Nevertheless we continue to feel very good about the underlying trends in our business. Our deal flow remains strong and we continue to benefit from better access to merchandise, growing relationships with vendors and our increased size and scale. As always, we will remain conservative yet confident in the way we plan the business and our full year outlook.
